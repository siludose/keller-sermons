## English Transcript / 英文原文

### Introduction: Jesus Our King

Christianity isn't just a spiritual practice or set of moral teachings. **At its heart, it's the person of Jesus actively pursuing us.** In today's teaching, Tim Keller impacts how Jesus actively seeks us, reveals truth to us, and calls us to Himself.

> "Why do the nations conspire and the people's plot in vain? The kings of the earth take their stand and the rulers gather together against the Lord and against his anointed one."

### Part 1: We have a King

#### 1. The True King

God says, here's all these kings around, but there's a king above the kings. There's a king behind all the kings. There's my king, the true king that I have installed.

- **The literature of the human race is filled with legends of a great king.**
- These legends reflect a memory trace of a true king.

> "The hands of the king are healing hands and thus shall the rightful king be known."

### Part 2: We hate the King

#### 1. Human Rebellion

The natural heart of every human being hates the true king.

- **"I am my own" is the central conviction of hell.**
- We hate the idea of a king who has rights over us.

> "Let us break their chains, they say, and throw off their fetters."

### Part 3: We need the King

#### 1. The Need for Submission

Verses 10, 11, and 12 say, there's no in between. You can either serve and rejoice and kiss the king, and you'll find a refuge in him and be blessed.

- **There is no refuge from the king, only refuge in the king.**
- The yoke eventually becomes a refuge.

> "Blessed are all who take refuge in Him."

### Conclusion

To treat Jesus as a king, you must obey, submit, rely, and expect.

- Obey: Always forgive, always tell the truth.
- Submit: Accept the circumstances of your life.
- Rely: Kiss the son, meaning to love him above all.
- Expect: Rejoice with trembling, have expectations worthy of a king.

> "There's no refuge from him, but there's a tremendous refuge in him."

Let's pray.

Father, we ask now that as we think about these things, because it's your word, that your spirit would come and open our hearts to how we should be applying these things. Amen.

## 中文翻译

### 引言：耶稣，我们的君王

基督教不仅仅是一种灵修实践或道德教导。**其核心是耶稣这个人积极地追求我们。** 在今天的教导中，Tim Keller 讲述了耶稣如何积极地寻找我们，向我们揭示真理，并呼召我们归向祂。

> “为什么列国争闹，万民谋算虚妄的事？世上的君王一齐起来，臣宰一同商议，要敌挡耶和华并祂的受膏者。”

### 第一部分：我们有一位君王

#### 1. 真正的君王

上帝说，这里有许多君王，但有一位君王高于众王。有一位君王在所有君王之上。祂是我所立的真君王。

- **人类文学中充满了关于伟大君王的传说。**
- 这些传说反映了对真正君王的记忆痕迹。

> “君王的手是医治的手，因此应当认识这位合法的君王。”

### 第二部分：我们憎恨这位君王

#### 1. 人类的反叛

每个人的自然心态都憎恨这位真君王。

- **“我是我自己的”是地狱的核心信念。**
- 我们憎恨有权利支配我们的君王的想法。

> “我们要挣开他们的捆绑，脱去他们的绳索。”

### 第三部分：我们需要这位君王

#### 1. 需要顺服

第10、11和12节说，没有中间地带。你可以选择侍奉、欢喜、亲吻君王，并在祂里面找到避难所和祝福。

- **没有从君王那里逃避的避难所，只有在君王里面的避难所。**
- 轭最终成为避难所。

> “凡投靠祂的，都是有福的。”

### 结论

要将耶稣视为君王，你必须服从、顺服、依赖和期待。

- 服从：总是宽恕，总是说真话。
- 顺服：接受你生活中的环境。
- 依赖：亲吻儿子，意味着爱祂胜过一切。
- 期待：战兢而欢喜，对君王有配得上的期待。

> “没有从祂那里逃避的避难所，但在祂里面有极大的避难所。”

让我们祷告。

父啊，我们现在请求，当我们思考这些事情时，因为这是你的话语，愿你的灵来开启我们的心，帮助我们应用这些教导。阿门。
