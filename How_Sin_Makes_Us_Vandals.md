## English Transcript / 英文原文

### Introduction: The Fundamental Problem

Some people say the fundamental problem of the world is poverty. Others say it's bad systems, poor education, or biology, but what if none of these can fully explain the brokenness we see, both in the headlines and in our own hearts? In today's teaching, Tim Keller looks at how the Bible's teaching on sin gives us a deeply honest and yet incredibly hopeful view of the world.

> "What's wrong with us? Why the selfishness, the cruelty, the corruption, the crime, the racism, the injustice? Why? Where does it come from?"

**The Bible's answer is ancient and it's profound and that is sin in the human heart.**

### Part One: The Nature of Sin

#### 1. Multidimensionality of Sin

One of the reasons why it's so widely rejected, I believe, is actually because what's not understood is that the biblical understanding and doctrine of sin is multidimensional.

- Sin is breaking God's law and therefore we're guilty and condemned.
- Sin is more than just breaking rules; it's a deep-seated condition.

### Part Two: Sin's Impact on Us

#### 1. The Banality of Evil

What do you think sin really does to you in its advanced stages? What does sin really do to the human heart?

> "The most advanced, the most prominent, the most characteristic, effective sin on the human heart is not to make us bad but boring."

**Sin makes you mediocre.**

### Part Three: Sin's Impact on God

#### 1. A Reduced God

What does sin lead us to do toward God?

> "She has whittled God down, she has made him, she has put a handle on him. This is a God you can put in your purse."

**A little God, a manageable tamed God will make you a little manageable, controllable person.**

### Conclusion: The Cure for Sin

#### 1. The Image of God

Why is it that God said no image?

> "Jesus Christ is the image of the invisible God."

**Only when you have a God like that, only when you admit that Jesus Christ is the image of God that he died for me, that is what finally shows me a greatness beyond anything I would know otherwise.**

Let's pray.

## 中文翻译

### 引言：根本问题

有些人说世界的根本问题是贫穷。其他人说是糟糕的制度、教育不良或生物学，但如果这些都不能完全解释我们在头条新闻和自己内心中看到的破碎呢？在今天的教导中，Tim Keller 看圣经关于罪的教导如何给我们一个深刻诚实但又充满希望的世界观。

> “我们怎么了？为什么自私、残酷、腐败、犯罪、种族主义、不公正？为什么？它从哪里来？”

**圣经的答案是古老而深刻的，那就是人心中的罪。**

### 第一部分：罪的本质

#### 1. 罪的多维性

我相信，罪之所以被广泛拒绝，是因为人们不理解圣经对罪的多维理解和教义。

- 罪是违反上帝的律法，因此我们有罪并被定罪。
- 罪不仅仅是违反规则；它是一种根深蒂固的状况。

### 第二部分：罪对我们的影响

#### 1. 恶的平庸性

你认为罪在其高级阶段对你真正做了什么？罪对人类心灵真正做了什么？

> “罪对人类心灵最先进、最显著、最具特征的影响不是让我们变坏，而是变得无聊。”

**罪使你平庸。**

### 第三部分：罪对上帝的影响

#### 1. 一个被缩小的上帝

罪使我们对上帝的态度如何？

> “她把上帝缩小了，她给他加了个把手。这是一个你可以放在钱包里的上帝。”

**一个小的、可控的被驯服的上帝会让你成为一个小的、可控的人。**

### 结论：罪的解药

#### 1. 上帝的形象

为什么上帝说不要造偶像？

> “耶稣基督是无形上帝的形象。”

**只有当你拥有这样的上帝，只有当你承认耶稣基督是上帝的形象并为我而死，这才最终向我展示了超越我所知的一切的伟大。**

让我们祷告。
