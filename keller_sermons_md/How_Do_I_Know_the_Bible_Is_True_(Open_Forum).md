## English Transcript / 英文原文

### Introduction: The Challenge of Public Faith

How comfortable are you when it comes to being open about your faith? This month on the podcast, Tim Keller looks at what the Bible says about having a public faith. He shows us what it looks like to be open about our faith in a pluralistic society in a way that creates civility and peace and meaningful dialogue with our neighbors.

### The Common Skepticism About the Bible

It is very common for a lot of people, even people who want to live the Christian life to feel that they've got to check their brains at the door. A lot of people will say to me, I know that probably Christianity isn't really true, but I want it to be true or I live a general Christian life, but I know that there's all sorts of problems with the historical accounts.

> "If you go to college, generally in religion 101 or philosophy 101, if you read the newspaper, you'll get this basic approach to the Bible."

**Many years after the event, somebody, some gospel writers wrote down these accounts.**

### The Historical Reliability of the Gospels

Number one, if you honestly look at what the gospels claim, you will see they're not written as legends or fiction, they are either historical accounts or a deliberate hoax.

- The gospels claim to be historical documents.
- They were written within the lifetime of eyewitnesses.

### Tests for Historical Documents

There are several rules, and if you give those tests to the Bible, it comes out as a reliable historical document too.

- **Bibliographic Test:** Hundreds of copies within a few decades of the originals.
- **Genre Test:** The gospels claim to be historical accounts.
- **Corroboration Test:** Other historical witnesses corroborate the testimony.

### The Problem of Miracles

If you say miracles can't happen, and you obviate an account of the evidence of a miracle before you even read it, you're arguing in a circle.

> "As long as you're willing not to come with a kind of unscientific, anti-logical, dogmatic assertion that if there's miracles in this account, it can't be historical."

### Conclusion: The Implications of Jesus' Claims

C.S. Lewis years ago said, and he wasn't the first one. He says, there's only four possible ways to explain Jesus. He was either a legend, a liar, a lunatic, or Lord.

**The reason I'm a Christian, one of the main reasons is because as crazy as it is that he actually might be the son of God, it actually makes less sense that either the other two alternatives would be true.**

## 中文翻译

### 引言：公开信仰的挑战

当谈到公开你的信仰时，你有多自在？本月的播客中，Tim Keller 探讨了圣经对公开信仰的看法。他向我们展示了在多元化社会中如何以一种创造文明、和平和有意义的对话方式公开我们的信仰。

### 对圣经的普遍怀疑

很多人，即使是那些想要过基督徒生活的人，常常觉得他们必须在门口放下他们的理智。很多人会对我说，我知道基督教可能不是真的，但我希望它是真的，或者我过着一般的基督徒生活，但我知道历史记载中有各种问题。

> “如果你上大学，一般在宗教101或哲学101课上，如果你读报纸，你会得到这种对圣经的基本看法。”

**在事件发生多年后，有人，一些福音书作者写下了这些记载。**

### 福音书的历史可靠性

首先，如果你诚实地看福音书的主张，你会发现它们不是作为传奇或虚构写的，它们要么是历史记载，要么是故意的骗局。

- 福音书声称是历史文件。
- 它们是在目击者的生命期内写成的。

### 历史文献的测试

有几个规则，如果你对圣经进行这些测试，它也会被视为可靠的历史文件。

- **书目测试：** 在原件的几十年内有数百份副本。
- **类型测试：** 福音书声称是历史记载。
- **证实测试：** 其他历史见证证实了这些证词。

### 奇迹的问题

如果你说奇迹不可能发生，并且在你甚至阅读之前就否定了奇迹的证据，你就是在循环论证。

> “只要你愿意不带着一种不科学、反逻辑、教条式的断言，即如果这个记载中有奇迹，它就不能是历史的。”

### 结论：耶稣主张的影响

C.S. Lewis 多年前说过，他不是第一个。他说，解释耶稣只有四种可能的方式。他要么是传奇，要么是骗子，要么是疯子，要么是主。

**我成为基督徒的原因之一是，因为尽管他实际上可能是上帝的儿子这件事听起来很疯狂，但其他两种选择更不合理。**
